2. What does the California Assessment of Student Performance and Progress do?



The CAASPP is California’s new statewide student assessment system, replacing the STAR tests. It includes the Smarter Balanced, alternate and science assessments.

3. Where do you get information for achievement level descriptors?



On the SBAC website — www.smarterbalanced.org.

4. What’s the best way to use achievement level descriptors?



Achievement level descriptors can help educators understand how cut scores, or achievement levels, were determined. There are four levels of descriptors, which serve as starting points for discussion about the performance of individual students in mathematics and English language arts. Educators can use ALDs to design assessments and to inform instruction.

5. How do you get access to the Smarter Balanced Digital Library?



School districts and local education authorities are to provide a password to teachers so they can access the Smarter Balanced Digital Library.

6. What’s the difference between an assessment and a test?



An assessment is the process of documenting knowledge, skills, attitudes and beliefs. A test is an assessment intended to measure respondents’ knowledge, content or other abilities.

7. What’s the difference between formative and summative assessments?



Formative assessment is geared to inform instruction, recognizing how students are understanding content. Summative exams show the outcome of a course of study.

8. What is the timeline for administering the SBAC summative assessments?



The SBAC summative assessments are to be administered during a 12-week window prior to the end of the semester. Get details from the California Department of Education.

9. What kind of information will students and parents receive after taking the SBAC assessment?



Individual scores that will not be organized for comparative purposes. Keep in mind California’s “accountability holiday” lasts through 2017.

10. What kind of information will the district receive about students?



Individual student results. The results are intended to inform instruction and are not for comparison purposes.
